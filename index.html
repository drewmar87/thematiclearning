An Authentic Assessment for the students in Dental Hygiene
The goal of school is to create long lasting learning outcomes that the students can use the rest of their lives.

With this in mind we have to decribe the bigger picture as it relates to Dental Hygiene - specifically Radiology; using a backwards design process.

  1. Identify “big picture” learning outcomes (what do you want learners to remember in 10 years?)
    1. I would like the learners to remember specifically how to mount radiographs.
    2. I would like the learners to remember certain anatomy and look for any variations from normal.
    3. I would like the learners to be able to identify any pathological anomalies.
  2. Creating a product to show evidence of this learning:
    1. The Students are going to create a readiographic case study using their choice of means.
      • The students are going to set up a series of radiographs from a medically involved patient that would show a radiographic abnormality.
      • From there create a made-up patient that meets those health criteria for the other students to figure out.
      • The participants will be required to identify all information pertinant to the patient through the series of radiographs to determine the main pathological disease.
      • The students will then swap case studies to try and figure out pathological abnormalities that others have created.
The purpose of this assignment is to help the students not only use critical thinking on others' projects to discover the abnormality. But instead use their own backwards design process to do just the same as we did to further solidify the learning by having the student create something using the technology available at this time (see: PICRAT and Backwards Design).
A technological moral and ethical dilemma.
Background:
Case Story: Dystopian Health Care, The pure vileness of insurance companies.

Genre: Dystopian

Characters:

Dentist: Dr. Phearsome

Patient: Mytoo thurts (Sales associate in a mattress firm)

Insurance Company: Medicaid

Introduction:

Patient Mytoo Thurts, enters the public health dental clinic in Las Cruces, NM due to a crazy amount of pain in his front tooth. Every time Mytoo tries to bite down there's a shooting sharp pain, much to be described as a lightning bolt going up the front of his face.

Mytoo: Excuse me, I'm a walk-in patient here to see Dr. Phearsome, I have a lot of pain in my front tooth.

Receptionist: Do you have insurance?

Mytoo: Well, unfortunately my job doesn't offer insurance but I have Medicaid through the US government!

Receptionist: Please wait over there in the basic economy section of the waiting room.

*Mytoo turns and sees a section of the waiting room much darker than the rest, unknown liquids dripping from the broken chairs, a couple people who seem to be in and out of consciousness around him.>

*Four Hours Pass*

Receptionist: Dr. Phearsome will see you now.

*Dr. Phearsome does his clinical analysis of the tooth and comes up with the diagnosis*

Dr. Phearsome: Your front tooth has a periapical abscess, left untreated the tooth with fester and go systemic, possibly killing you.

Dr. Phearsome: Best course of treatment is for a root canal with a crown afterwards to uphold the integrity of the tooth.

Dr. Phearsome: Unfortunately, the only course of treatment Medicaid will provide is to have the tooth pulled, saving the tooth is seen as an esthetic feature and therefore not covered.

*Mytoo panics, he speaks to customers every day, he can't lose this tooth!*

Mytoo: I pay my taxes, my bills, I do what I'm supposed to do day in and out and the government won't pay for me to fix my front tooth?? I need the tooth my livelihood depends on it!

*Mytoo starts to weep.* *The technology to save his tooth exists, yet the government refuses to pay for it to save the livelihood of a patient.*

*Dr. Phearsome looks at the clock, nearing 5:00-ending time*

Dr. Phearsome: *sigh....*

Dr. Phearsome: Assistant, please get us prepped for a root canal and crown. And please place the code and charges under an "extraction".

*Mytoo looks up*

Dr. Phearsome: The government will long see us dead if it would make them another dollar. Please don't say anything about this.

*Dr. Phearsome performs the procedure and the tooth comes out beautifully and saved.*

Mytoo: Thank you Doctor, I have no idea how I can repay you!

Dr. Phearsome: Don't mention it.

Conclusion

*The year is 2022, not a dystopian future, and this is the current state of our Government Sponsored dental care. The government does not care about you and will sacrifice you and your wellbeing to save a dollar. Government funded health insurance sounds great in theory, in practice- unless you will die, you are just esthetic and therefore not covered.*

Section 6, The aftermath
Questions to Consider:
  1. Did Dr. Phearsome commit fraud in this situation?
  2. What would you have done in this situation as the Dr.?
  3. Is it okay to smudge the rules when it comes to the government?
  4. What repurcussions could Dr. Phearsome face if this is found out?
  5. Think about the ethics and morals related to the story

      6. Writing Prompt: Patient Mytoo was actually secretly an undercover representative of the Medicaid government office. Write about the aftermath and the internal emotional and moral turmoil that Mytoo will struggle through and if he decides to report this situation to the higher-ups. What would you do? Why would you do or not do it? The ending to this story is yours.
